Detailed Description

Aggregated counters and (optional) unique-client tracking.

  • Hot path: packet/byte counters are std::atomic and updated with memory_order_relaxed to avoid locks and minimize contention.
  • Unique clients: tracked in an unordered_map guarded by a small std::mutex. This should be called less frequently than the hot-path counters to avoid contention in tight loops.
Thread-safety
  • inc_sent, inc_recv, add_rx_bytes, add_tx_bytes and the getters are lock-free and thread-safe.
  • note_client and unique_clients acquire an internal mutex.
Consistency
Reads of different counters are not atomic as a group; a single to_string call may reflect slightly different instants per counter. This is generally fine for diagnostics and metrics.
udp::Stats s;
s.inc_recv(64);
s.add_rx_bytes(64 * 128);
s.note_client(0x7f000001 /*127.0.0.1*/, 9000);
std::string line = s.to_string(); // "recv=64 sent=0 unique_clients=1 rx_bytes=8192 tx_bytes=0"

uint16_t  port 
)
inline

Record (or update) activity for a specific client (addr, port).

Increments an internal hit counter for the given ClientKey. If the key was not present, inserts it with a count of 1.

Note
This method acquires a short-lived mutex to protect the map. Prefer calling it at a reduced frequency (e.g., sampling) in very hot loops.
